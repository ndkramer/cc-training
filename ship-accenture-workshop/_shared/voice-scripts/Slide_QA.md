## Professor

Here's the key insight about Q&A slides: silence in the first five seconds does not mean the room has no questions — it means nobody wants to go first. Your job is to break that friction. The mechanism is simple: prime the room with the question you already know they have, answer it yourself, and that gives everyone else permission to follow.

Notice that the four prompts at the bottom are not decorative — they map directly to the objections that surface in every enterprise room: pricing, security posture, language support, and deployment model. What this tells us is that you should have answered those four questions in your head before you walk in.

If the room skews technical or power-user, the questions will shift toward cost controls and audit trails — those are signs of a sophisticated buyer. The reason this matters is that those audiences want specificity, not reassurance. Be ready to point at /effort for the cost-control question and the EnterpriseControls slide for the audit question. Don't wait for them to dig — surface the reference proactively and you signal that you know the product at depth.
