## Professor

[Dark slide. Hold for a beat before speaking — the visual break does work.]

I want to be direct about this, because it's the thing that separates teams that get dramatic results from teams that get mediocre ones.

Claude Code is as good as the context you give it. That's not a soft claim — it's a mechanical one. The model has no persistent knowledge of your codebase between sessions. It has no access to your conventions, your architectural decisions, your domain vocabulary, unless you provide that context explicitly. Skip that setup and you get a generic answer from a model that's reasoning about an abstract codebase that happens to resemble yours. Set it up properly and you get a model that reasons about your specific system, with your constraints, in your language.

The reason this matters is the economics of a token budget. Every token you spend on well-structured context — CLAUDE.md, skills, MCP — is a token that prevents a wrong answer you'd have to throw out and retry. Poorly scoped context isn't neutral. It's a tax on every subsequent exchange. Everything in this section is about closing the gap between 'model that knows nothing about {{vehicle}}' and 'model that behaves like a senior engineer who's been on the team for six months.'
