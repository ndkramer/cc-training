## Professor

[Auto-filters — only shows checklist items for slides that were actually in this deck.]

The instinct on a recap slide is to read the list. Resist it. The list is for the slide; the closing is for the room.

Notice what the recap is really doing structurally: it's the moment where you compress ninety minutes of material into the two or three things that follow them into Monday. Reading every checkbox dilutes that compression. Picking the two or three that landed hardest concentrates it.

The reliable winners, almost regardless of audience, are CLAUDE.md, Plan Mode, and whichever demo got the most lean-in. CLAUDE.md because it's the cheapest behavior change with the highest return; Plan Mode because it makes Claude safe to apply to scary changes; the lean-in demo because that's the room telling you which capability mapped to their real work.

Close like a friend giving advice, not a slide summary. "If you remember nothing else: write a CLAUDE.md, use Shift+Tab before scary changes, and try a skill." That's enough for Monday — and Monday is what determines whether anything you said today actually changes how they work.
